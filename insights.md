# Healthcare Patient Appointment No-Show Analysis - Insights

## Summary

This project analyzed 110,521 valid healthcare appointment records to understand patient no-show behavior. The dashboard shows that 22,319 appointments were missed, giving an overall no-show rate of about 20.19%.

## Main Insights

### 1. Overall No-Show Rate

The overall no-show rate is approximately 20.19%, meaning around 1 out of every 5 appointments was missed.

### 2. Age Group Pattern

Teen and young adult patients show higher no-show rates compared with senior patients. This suggests younger patient groups may need stronger follow-up communication or more flexible scheduling options.

### 3. Waiting Period Pattern

Appointments with longer waiting periods have higher observed no-show rates. Patients scheduled far in advance may forget, lose interest, resolve the issue, or face scheduling conflicts before the appointment date.

### 4. SMS Reminder Pattern

Patients who received SMS reminders show a higher observed no-show rate. This should be interpreted carefully. It does not mean SMS reminders caused no-shows. A more likely explanation is that reminders may have been sent to patients or appointments already considered higher risk.

### 5. Weekday Pattern

The appointment weekday chart shows some variation. Saturday appears to have a higher no-show rate in the dashboard, while weekday differences are smaller than the differences seen across age groups and waiting periods.

### 6. Neighborhood Pattern

Some neighborhoods show high observed no-show rates, but this may be affected by low appointment counts. A neighborhood with very few appointments can show an unusually high percentage if only a small number of patients missed appointments.

## Business Recommendations

1. Focus reminder efforts on appointments with long waiting periods.
2. Create targeted reminder campaigns for teen and young adult patients.
3. Review the SMS reminder process and compare reminder effectiveness across patient groups.
4. Use weekday trends to support scheduling and staffing decisions.
5. Review neighborhood no-show rates together with appointment volume before making operational decisions.

## Limitation

This analysis is based on observational appointment data. The dashboard can identify patterns and associations, but it cannot prove that one factor directly causes no-shows.
